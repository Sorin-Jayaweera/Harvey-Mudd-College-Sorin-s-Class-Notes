
$$
\begin{align}
T = \frac{1}{A+B\ln (R)+C[\ln (R)]^{3}}
\end{align}
$$
“T” is in degrees Kelvin and “A”, “B”, and “C” are coefficients for the thermistor

$$
\begin{align}
\begin{bmatrix}
 1 & \ln R_{1} & \ln ^{3}R_{1} \\
 1 & \ln R_{2} & \ln ^{3}R_{2} \\
 1 & \ln R_{3} & \ln ^{3}R_{3} \\
\end{bmatrix}  \begin{bmatrix}
A \\ B  \\ C
\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}
\frac{1}{T_{1}} \\ \frac{1}{T_{2}} \\ \frac{1}{T_{3}}
\end{bmatrix}
\end{align}
$$
